Business Time is a small, off-the-record dinner series in Auckland. A few times a year we gather a room of operators, founders and chief executives around a single guest — and let the conversation run.

Reserve a seat How it works

The format

Less stage. More table.

No panels. No microphones. No name tags. Just twenty or so business leaders, a single guest of honour, and the kind of long, unhurried conversation that doesn't happen at conferences.

Dinner runs three courses. The guest opens with ten minutes on something they're thinking about, then opens the floor. Everything said in the room stays in the room.

  • Twenty seats. One long table, no head.
  • Two hours of Q&A. Guided lightly, never rushed.
  • Chatham House. Off the record by default.

The venue

Non Solo Pizza, Parnell.

A quiet back room at one of Auckland's most loved Italian restaurants. The food is unfussy and excellent — the kitchen knows us, the wine list is deep, and the room was built for the kind of evening we throw.

Drinks from 6:30pm. Seated by 7:00pm. Most nights wrap a little after 10.
